CALCULATION

Most coaches watch the game. Few actually see it.

On the sideline, it is easy to be seduced by the noise. A bad bounce, a missed call, a lucky deflection. We tell ourselves that soccer is random. We tell ourselves, "We were unlucky."

This is the comforting lie of the Unexamined Game.

If you believe the result is random, you absolve yourself of responsibility. If you believe the result is fate, you stop looking for the variables you can control.

The Examined Game is different.

To examine the game is to strip away the emotion and look at the integers. It is not "bad luck"; it is Variance. It is not "heart"; it is Momentum. It is not "destiny"; it is Probability.

We build tools not to predict the future, but to discipline the present. To force us to assign a number to a "gut feeling." To weigh experience against talent.

Socrates said the unexamined life is not worth living. The unexamined game is not worth playing.

Stop watching. Start calculating.
